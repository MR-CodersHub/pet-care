Signs Your Senior Pet Needs a Vet Visit

Dr. Sarah Jenkins

Lead Veterinarian

As our pets enter their golden years, their needs change. While "slowing down" is a natural part of aging, it can also mask underlying medical issues. Early detection is key to ensuring your senior companion remains comfortable and happy.

Changes in Appetite or Thirst

A sudden increase or decrease in appetite can signal dental issues, kidney disease, or diabetes. Similarly, if your pet is drinking significantly more water than usual, it's time for a check-up.

Lumps and Bumps

Make it a habit to regularly check your pet's body for any new growths. While many lumps in senior pets are benign lipomas (fatty tumors), only a veterinarian can confirm this through a fine-needle aspirate.

Watch the Gait

Is your pet struggling to stand up, avoid stairs, or no longer jumping onto the couch? These are often signs of arthritis, which can be managed with medication and lifestyle adjustments.

Bad Breath

While "dog breath" is a common trope, exceptionally foul odor can indicate severe periodontal disease or even internal issues like kidney failure. Dental health is closely linked to heart and organ health in senior pets.

Behavioral Changes

Confusion, irritability, or changes in sleep patterns can be signs of Cognitive Dysfunction Syndrome (CDS), often referred to as "pet dementia." There are supplements and medications that can help improve cognitive function.
