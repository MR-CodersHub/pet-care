Spring Safety: Toxic Garden Plants

Dr. Sarah Jenkins

Lead Veterinarian

As you start planning your spring garden, it's essential to consider the safety of your feline friends. Many common garden plants and flowers are highly toxic to cats if ingested. Here's a list of plants to avoid or keep out of reach.

1. Lilies (Lilium and Hemerocallis)

Lilies are perhaps the most dangerous plants for cats. Even a small amount of pollen or water from a vase can cause fatal kidney failure in cats. If you have cats, it's best to avoid lilies entirely, both indoors and in the garden.

2. Tulips and Hyacinths

While the flowers are beautiful, the bulbs of tulips and hyacinths contain the highest concentration of toxins. Ingestion can lead to severe gastrointestinal upset, drooling, and even heart abnormalities.

Emergency Contact

If you suspect your cat has ingested a toxic plant, contact your veterinarian or a pet poison control center immediately. Don't wait for symptoms to appear.

3. Azaleas and Rhododendrons

Even just a few leaves can cause vomiting, diarrhea, and drooling. In severe cases, ingestion can lead to coma or death. These plants are very common in landscaping, so be sure to check your yard.

4. Oleander

Oleander is highly toxic to almost all animals, including cats and humans. It contains cardiac glycosides that can cause severe heart arrhythmias and fatal heart failure.
