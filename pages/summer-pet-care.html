10 Tips for Keeping Your Dog Cool This Summer

Dr. Sarah Jenkins

Lead Veterinarian

As the mercury rises, the risks to our canine companions increase. Dogs don't sweat like humans do; they primarily cool themselves through panting and releasing heat through their paw pads. This makes them much more susceptible to heatstroke and dehydration.

1. Never Leave Your Dog in a Parked Car

Even with the windows cracked, the temperature inside a car can reach 120 degrees in just minutes on a 70-degree day. On an 85-degree day, it can reach 102 degrees in 10 minutes and 120 degrees in 30 minutes. Heatstroke can be fatal in minutes.

2. Provide Constant Access to Fresh Water

Hydration is key. Make sure your dog has multiple water bowls available, both indoors and outdoors. Add ice cubes to their water to keep it cool longer, or invest in a pet water fountain to encourage drinking.

3. Limit Exercise During Peak Heat

Walk your dog in the early morning or late evening when temperatures are cooler. Avoid strenuous activities during the hottest part of the day (usually 10 AM to 4 PM).

The 7-Second Rule

Place the back of your hand on the pavement. If you can't hold it there for 7 seconds, it's too hot for your dog's paws. Walk on the grass instead!

4. Provide Plenty of Shade

If your dog spends time outdoors, ensure they have access to a shaded area. A doghouse is not ideal as it traps heat; instead, look for a spot under a tree or a canopy that allows for airflow.

5. Use Cooling Mats and Damp Towels

Cooling mats or even a damp towel for your dog to lie on can help lower their body temperature. You can also gently mist them with cool (not ice-cold) water.
